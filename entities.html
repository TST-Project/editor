Tamil transcription

Vowel signs

Although normally, vowel signs are attached to a consonant, there are cases where you may want to input a vowel sign independent of a consonant. If you are transcribing in Tamil script, this can be achieved by typing a space followed by the vowel sign.

Alternatively, there are two named glyphs, for kāl and kompu, which can be entered as XML tags.

<g ref="#ra_r_kal"/>→ஆ
<g ref="#kompu"/>→எ

When you are transcribing in Roman transliteration, and wish to indicate that a vowel sign has been added or deleted, you can use the following pattern:

RomanTamil
<subst>k<del>e</del><add>ā</add></subst>→keāஎகஆ
<subst>k<del>e</del><add>a</add></subst>→keaஎக

final -m ligature

In manuscripts, a final -m may be combined with the previous consonant as a conjunct character. See https://tst.hypotheses.org/final-m for examples.

When transcribing in Tamil script, this ligature can be produced by inserting a zero-width joiner (U+200D) between the consonant and the final -m. Alternatively, this can be transcribed using an XML tag.

RomanTamil
<g ref="#nna=m"/>→ணம்
<g ref="#nna=m">ṇam</g>→ṇamணம்
<g ref="#ya=m"/>→யம்
<g ref="#ya=m">yam</g>→yamயம்

Tamil oṃkāra

To differentiate the oṃkāra symbol from the o vowel with an anusvāra, you can transliterate it as oṁ.

oṁ→ௐ
oṃ→ஒஂ
om→ஒம்
